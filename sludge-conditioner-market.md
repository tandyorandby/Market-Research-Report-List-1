<p><p>The sludge conditioner market is segmented into four primary types: </p><p>1. **Activated Sludge**: This involves treating wastewater with microorganisms that digest organic matter, producing sludge as a byproduct.</p><p>  </p><p>2. **Primary Sludge**: This is the solid material that settles out during the initial wastewater treatment process, containing high organic content.</p><p>3. **Mixed Sludge**: This consists of a blend of primary and activated sludge, optimizing the treatment process.</p><p>4. **Other**: This category includes specialized sludge types from various treatments or industrial processes. These distinctions help tailor conditioning methods to improve sludge management and reuse.</p></p>
